1. Non-psychiatric conditions

  • Physical disorders with symptoms that overlap with those of the schizophrenia prodrome are rare, and nearly all can be ruled out with a thorough history, physical examination (including neurological examination), laboratory studies, and neuroimaging or EEG studies.

1.1. Metabolic disorders

  • Renal dysfunction [S. Creat, BUN, more, Case report 1, Case report 2]
  • Hepatic failure [LFT, S. Ammonia, INR, Viral markers, more 1, more 2]
  • Pancreatic disease [S. Lipase/Amylase, more]
  • Hypernatremia/hyponatremia [S. Na++, S. Osm, Urine Osm, more-hypo, more-hyper]
  • Hypercalcemia/hypocalcemia [S. Ca++, S.PTH, Vit-D, more-hypo, more-hyper]
  • Hyperglycemia/hypoglycemia [RBS, more-hyper, more-hypo]
  • Porphyria [Urine for porphobilinogen, more]
  • Dehydration [dry mucosa, skin turgor, BUN]
  • Hyperosmolarity [S. Osm, DKA, HONK, Myxedema, more-HONK, more-DKA]

1.2. Endocrine disorders

  • Addison disease
  • Cushing disease
  • Hypothyroidism/hyperthyroidism
  • Hyperparathyroidism Panhypopituitarism

1.3. Nutritional deficiency states

  • Thiamin deficiency
  • Folate deficiency
  • B 12 deficiency
  • Niacin deficiency

1.4. Autoimmune disorders

  • Systemic lupus erythematosus
  • Temporal arteritis

1.5. Motor disorders

  • Parkinson disease
  • Wilson disease
  • Huntington disease
  • Sydenham chorea
  • Idiopathic basal ganglia calcification
  • Spinocerebellar degeneration

1.6. Myelin diseases

  • Adrenoleukodystrophy
  • Metachromatic leukodystrophy
  • Marchiafava-Bignami disease
  • Multiple sclerosis

1.7. Substance-induced disorders

  • Street drugs (including alcohol, hallucinogens, heroin, inhalants, psychostimulants, and phencyclidine)
  • Prescription drugs (including steroids and stimulants)
  • Withdrawal from alcohol, hallucinogens, opiates, psychostimulants, or sedative-hypnotics
  • Poisoning (e.g., with anticholinergics, carbon monoxide, or heavy metals)

1.8. Infections

  • Herpes simplex
  • HIV
  • Syphilis
  • Parasites

1.9. Miscellaneous conditions

  • Neoplasm
  • Cerebrovascular accident
  • Trauma (especially to frontal and temporal regions)
  • Inborn errors of metabolism
  • Seizure (especially complex partial seizures)
  • Hydrocephalus
  • Hypoxic encephalopathy
  • Narcolepsy
