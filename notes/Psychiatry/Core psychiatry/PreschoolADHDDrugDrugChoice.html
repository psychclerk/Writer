**Which medication is better for preschool children with ADHD?** Harstad et al [JAMA 2021;325:2067-2075](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33946100/) Access date 20/6/21 # Background: - ADHD diagnosed in 2.4% pre-school age children - Stimulants are recommended as first line agents - However, up to 25% of preschool-age children with ADHD are treated with α2-adrenergic agonist medications # Objective - To determine the frequency of reported improvement in ADHD symptoms and adverse effects associated with α2-adrenergic agonists and stimulant medication for initial ADHD medication treatment in preschool-age children # Methods - Retroscpetive EMR records review - 7 outpatient developmental-behavioral pediatric practices - 2017-2019 # Important results - Improvement profile - Efficacy of both comparable [66% improved with α2-agnoist; 78% improved with stimulants] - Side effect Profile - Daytime sleepiness more common with α2-agnoist (38% v/s 3%) - Moodiness irritability, appetite supression and difficulty sleeping more common with Stimulants (50% v/s 29%, 38% v/s 7%, 21% v/s 11%) # Conclusions - Differing side effects profile between medications - Efficacy comparision required further RCT # Comment - α2-agnoist may be better to use considering the side effect profile
